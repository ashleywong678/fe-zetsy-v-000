About

Mary Wanesia is Jeweler, an Arizona native, and a member of the Navajo tribe. She specializes in creating beautiful contemporary takes on the traditional adomments worn by Navajo in ceramonies and in daily life.

Jewelry in the Americans has an ancient hsitory. Beginning as far back as 8800 BCE, Paleo-Indians in the American Southwest drilled and shaped mulicolored stones and shells into beads and pendants. Olivella shell beads, dating from 6000 BCE, were found in Nevada; bone, antler, and possibly marine shell beads from 7000 BCE were found in Russell Cave in Alabama; copper jewelry was traded from Lake Superior beginning in 3000 BCE; and stone beads were carved in Poverty Point in Louisiana in 1500 BCE. Mary's jewelry has received recognition, and been featured in the National Museum of the American Indian, The New York TImes Style Magazine, and Sedona Monthly. The Collection has sold across the United States and Europe and been carried by the retail stores of Bergdorf Goodman.
